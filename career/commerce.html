Explore Careers in Commerce

Business and Management

BBA/BMS: This is a popular undergraduate course that prepares students for careers in management, leadership, and business operations.

MBA: An advanced degree that leads to senior management roles, specializing in areas like Finance, Marketing, Human Resources, and Operations.

Entrepreneurship: Starting your own business or working in a startup environment, where creativity and leadership skills are crucial.

Finance and Accounting

Chartered Accountant (CA): One of the most prestigious and sought-after careers in finance. It involves auditing, taxation, accounting, and consulting.

Company Secretary (CS): A professional responsible for ensuring that a company adheres to corporate governance norms, legal compliance, and business regulations.

Cost and Management Accountant (CMA): Specializing in cost analysis and management of business expenses and profitability.

Financial Analyst: Analyzing financial data to make informed business decisions, working in investment banking, wealth management, or corporate finance.

Actuarial Science: A career in risk management, where you analyze financial risks using mathematical and statistical methods, primarily in the insurance sector.

Banking and Insurance

Banking (PO or Clerk): Working in commercial, investment, or public sector banks in various roles such as Probationary Officer (PO) or Clerk.

Insurance: Careers in the insurance industry, such as insurance advisor, manager, or actuary, offering services in life, health, or general insurance.

Economics and Research

Economist: Specializing in economic analysis and working with government, think tanks, or financial institutions to predict economic trends and inform policy decisions.

Economic Analyst/Researcher: Conducting research in economics, finance, or market behavior for businesses, government agencies, or international organizations.

Law and Legal Studies

Law: Pursuing a five-year integrated law degree (BA LLB) to become a lawyer, with the option to specialize in corporate, criminal, civil, or international law.

Corporate Law: Specializing in the legal aspects of business transactions, mergers, and acquisitions, working in law firms or corporations.

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR): Focusing on patents, copyrights, trademarks, and other forms of intellectual property protection.

Marketing and Sales

Marketing Management: Developing strategies to promote products or services, with roles in brand management, advertising, and digital marketing.

Sales Management: Overseeing sales teams and strategies to increase revenue for companies in various industries, from retail to technology.

Hospitality and Tourism Management

Hotel Management: Managing hotel operations, guest services, event planning, and other aspects of the hospitality industry.

Tourism Management: Working in the travel industry, promoting destinations, and managing travel plans, tours, and events.
